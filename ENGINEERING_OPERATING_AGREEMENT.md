## 1. Purpose

The Engineering Operating Agreement (EOA) establishes the constitutional framework governing all engineering work performed within the Global Wakili repository.

It defines:

- engineering authority,
- governance obligations,
- contributor responsibilities,
- decision-making principles,
- architectural stewardship,
- certification obligations,
- and enforcement mechanisms.

This document supersedes individual engineering preferences, coding habits, or local conventions.

Every contribution is deemed to be made under this agreement.

---

## 2. Engineering Covenant

Every contributor agrees that their primary obligation is to protect the long-term integrity of the Global Wakili platform.

No contributor shall intentionally prioritize:

- speed over correctness,
- convenience over architecture,
- local optimization over system integrity,
- feature completion over platform stability,
- or personal preference over repository standards.

Engineering decisions shall always favor the enduring health of the platform.

---

## 3. Scope

This agreement governs all repository activities, including:

- Architecture
- Software Development
- Database Design
- Infrastructure
- Security
- DevOps
- Testing
- Documentation
- AI-assisted Engineering
- Code Review
- Release Engineering
- Operational Support
- Maintenance
- Refactoring
- Certification

It applies equally to new features, defect remediation, refactoring, migrations, and documentation updates.

---

## 4. Guiding Principles

All engineering work shall conform to the following principles:

### 4.1 Architecture First

Architecture shall guide implementation.

Implementation shall never redefine architecture without an approved architectural decision.

### 4.2 Correctness Before Velocity

Working software is insufficient.

Software must also be:

- correct,
- predictable,
- maintainable,
- secure,
- auditable,
- and certifiable.

### 4.3 Enterprise Quality

Every contribution shall be evaluated as though it will support:

- national law firms,
- multinational legal practices,
- regulatory audits,
- financial inspections,
- and decades of operational history.

### 4.4 Simplicity Through Discipline

Complexity shall only exist where it provides measurable value.

Accidental complexity shall be eliminated.

### 4.5 Explicit Design

Implicit behaviour creates operational risk.

Business rules, financial rules, and architectural decisions shall be explicit.

---

## 5. Governance Hierarchy

Engineering authority is exercised in the following order:

1. GW-EOS Governance Documents
2. Approved Architecture Decisions
3. Certified Repository Standards
4. Approved Technical Specifications
5. Individual Implementation Decisions

No implementation may contradict a higher authority.

---

## 6. Authority Structure

### Chief Architect

The Chief Architect is the final authority for:

- architecture,
- certification,
- governance,
- engineering standards,
- and repository direction.

The Chief Architect resolves architectural disputes.

### Engineering Contributors

Engineering contributors are responsible for:

- implementation,
- verification,
- documentation,
- risk identification,
- quality assurance,
- and continuous improvement.

Contributors shall not knowingly introduce technical debt without explicit approval through CHANGE_CONTROL.md.

### AI Engineering Agents

AI engineering agents shall be treated as engineering contributors.

They possess no independent architectural authority.

AI-generated changes require the same scrutiny and certification as human-authored contributions.

---

## 7. Engineering Responsibilities

Every contributor shall:

- preserve architectural integrity,
- respect module boundaries,
- maintain documentation,
- protect financial correctness,
- preserve auditability,
- enforce tenant isolation,
- improve maintainability,
- minimize operational risk.

Failure to satisfy these responsibilities constitutes non-compliance with GW-EOS.

---

## 8. Architectural Stewardship

Architecture is a shared responsibility.

Contributors shall actively identify:

- duplication,
- hidden coupling,
- layering violations,
- domain leakage,
- obsolete abstractions,
- inconsistent terminology,
- dependency inversion failures,
- scalability constraints.

Where practical, improvements should accompany functional work.

---

## 9. Financial Stewardship

Financial systems require heightened engineering discipline.

Contributors shall preserve:

- ledger immutability,
- balancing invariants,
- transaction traceability,
- reconciliation correctness,
- trust account segregation,
- accounting determinism.

Financial shortcuts are prohibited.

---

## 10. Legal Compliance

Engineering work shall preserve compliance obligations, including:

- audit evidence,
- client confidentiality,
- professional accountability,
- statutory retention,
- legal accounting practices,
- evidentiary integrity.

Engineering convenience shall never weaken legal compliance.

---

## 11. Security Obligations

Security is a mandatory engineering responsibility.

Every contributor shall evaluate:

- authentication,
- authorization,
- privilege boundaries,
- encryption,
- secrets management,
- audit logging,
- abuse prevention,
- secure defaults.

Security defects shall be treated as release blockers unless formally risk accepted.

---

## 12. Multi-Tenant Integrity

Global Wakili is a multi-tenant platform.

Every contributor shall ensure:

- tenant-aware queries,
- tenant-aware authorization,
- tenant-aware caching,
- tenant-aware background processing,
- tenant-aware reporting,
- tenant-aware notifications.

Cross-tenant data exposure is classified as a Critical Severity defect.

---

## 13. Quality Obligations

Every contribution shall satisfy the standards defined in:

- QUALITY_STANDARDS.md
- DEFINITION_OF_DONE.md
- CERTIFICATION_POLICY.md

Code that compiles but fails governance requirements shall not be considered complete.

---

## 14. Documentation Obligations

Documentation is part of the software system.

Contributors shall update governance documents whenever changes affect:

- architecture,
- workflows,
- responsibilities,
- operational procedures,
- certification criteria,
- release readiness.

Undocumented architectural change is prohibited.

---

## 15. Review Obligations

Every significant change shall undergo technical review.

Reviewers shall evaluate:

- correctness,
- architecture,
- consistency,
- security,
- performance,
- maintainability,
- documentation,
- operational impact.

Approval implies shared responsibility.

---

## 16. Engineering Ethics

Contributors shall:

- communicate honestly,
- disclose uncertainty,
- identify risks,
- avoid hidden assumptions,
- avoid misleading metrics,
- reject known unsafe implementations,
- acknowledge limitations.

Integrity is a professional obligation.

---

## 17. Decision Making

Engineering decisions shall be evidence-based.

When multiple approaches are viable, preference shall be given to the option that best improves:

1. architectural consistency,
2. operational simplicity,
3. long-term maintainability,
4. certification readiness,
5. platform resilience.

---

## 18. Technical Debt

Technical debt shall be:

- identified,
- documented,
- justified,
- prioritized,
- and scheduled.

Undocumented technical debt is governance debt.

Repeated acceptance of avoidable technical debt is considered a process failure.

---

## 19. Change Governance

No architectural change shall bypass the governance process defined in CHANGE_CONTROL.md.

All material changes shall include:

- rationale,
- impact assessment,
- migration strategy,
- rollback strategy,
- certification implications,
- documentation updates.

---

## 20. Certification Commitment

Every contributor agrees that engineering work is incomplete until it is capable of passing the certification requirements defined in CERTIFICATION_POLICY.md.

Completion is measured by evidence, not effort.

---

## 21. Non-Compliance

Examples of non-compliance include:

- bypassing architecture,
- introducing duplicate logic,
- weakening financial controls,
- omitting audit requirements,
- violating tenant isolation,
- undocumented breaking changes,
- inconsistent terminology,
- missing documentation,
- ignoring repository governance.

Non-compliance shall be corrected before certification.

---

## 22. Dispute Resolution

Where engineering disagreement exists, resolution shall follow this order:

1. Applicable GW-EOS document.
2. Approved Architecture Decision.
3. Repository precedent.
4. Certification requirements.
5. Chief Architect determination.

Personal preference shall not determine architectural outcomes.

---

## 23. Continuous Improvement

The repository shall continuously evolve toward:

- higher quality,
- lower operational risk,
- stronger governance,
- improved automation,
- clearer documentation,
- stronger security,
- greater financial integrity,
- increased maintainability.

Every contributor shares responsibility for this evolution.
